About MSK – Our Story

MSK Precision Engineering Group began in 2019 as a small workshop in Cherlapally, Hyderabad, focused on delivering accurate machining and fabrication with clear timelines and straightforward communication.

As customer needs evolved, MSK grew from a local job shop into an engineering-led manufacturing partner. Today we provide CNC machining, CAD/CAM, FEA/CFD, reverse engineering, rapid prototyping, additive manufacturing and digital quality systems for demanding applications.

We are building a new integrated facility at IP-Madharam while continuing to serve the wider Hyderabad industrial belt. Our team supports Defence, Aerospace, Oil & Gas, Marine & Offshore, Robotics, Automotive, Rail and Agriculture clients with an end-to-end workflow that connects design decisions to shop-floor reality.

MSK is not just a machine shop. We are an engineering company with manufacturing capability – sitting at the intersection of engineering intent, CNC precision and digital traceability. Our role is to translate specifications, loads and tolerances into stable, repeatable processes that can be defended with data and documentation.

  • CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics): internal/external flow, pressure drop, heat transfer, aerodynamics

  • Lean Methods: 5S, Kaizen, SMED, and standard work to reduce waste and variability

Data, Automation & Precision Engineering

Modern manufacturing needs more than machines – it needs connected information. At MSK, we treat data as another engineering input, alongside drawings, load cases and tolerances. We combine shop-floor data, engineering models and digital workflows to make our decisions more objective and our processes more stable.

  • Where the data comes from: CNC machine logs, CAM programs, tool sheets, inspection reports, non-conformances, FEA/CFD results and job histories. Over time, this builds a picture of which parts, features and processes are stable – and which are not.
  • In-house ERP/MES-style tracking: Internal tools that behave like a lightweight ERP/MES system for machining. Jobs move through digital travellers linking drawings, revisions, fixtures, programs, inspection plans and delivery milestones.
  • How data science is used: We look for patterns in tolerances, distortions, rework, cycle times and scrap. Focused statistical models and machine learning highlight where tighter control brings the biggest benefit.
  • Digital automation in the workflow: Automatic generation of inspection templates from CAD, pre-filled traveller documents, linking FEA/CFD envelopes to process notes, and flagging high-risk geometries based on past jobs.
  • Practical ML applications: Anomaly detection on inspection data, predicting dimensions likely to drift, clustering similar parts so successful process settings can be reused, and estimating cycle times from historical work.
  • Value for the client: More consistent quality, fewer surprises, clearer justification for DFM recommendations, and better predictability on cost and lead time.

In short, we use data science, ERP-style tracking and machine learning to support better engineering judgement – not to replace it.
